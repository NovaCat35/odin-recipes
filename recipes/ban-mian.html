Description

One of my favorite instant comfort foods is 拌面 (ban mian, meaning “tossed noodles”), a completely underappreciated and little-known street food from Fujian province. Ban mian is a totally generic term, and googling it yields a wide variety of noodle dishes. The Fujianese variant, however, is perhaps the most amenable to Americans: it involves peanut butter! And it takes about as much time to cook as instant ramen.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 ½ teaspoons oyster sauce
  • 1 ½ teaspoons sesame oil
  • Sugar, to taste
  • 2 (4 ounce) servings of wonton noodles or you noodles of choice

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to boil. Add noodles and cook according to package instructions
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil and sugar
  3. Once the noodles are cooked through, reserve 1/2 cup of the starchy water and add the drained cooked noodles to the bowl with the prepared sauce
  4. Toss to combine and use the starchy water to adjust the consistency
  5. Garnish with scallions, sesame seeds or chili oil
